Seamlessly Integrate Reflective Design Education Into Your Curriculum

The DLR is a free tool that allows instructors to search, access and contribute challenge problems, as well as archive and view the student-generated responses.

Search, Access, and Contribute Design Challenges

Design Challenges are open-ended problems, designed to allow students to learn through discovery. Based on the learning objectives of a specific course, these problems promote the independent exploration of a variety of design decisions without an instructor providing a correct answer.

Administer Design Challenges to Your Courses

The DLR allows instructors to conduct activities for their courses. These activities have an associated Design Challenge, which can be administered to students for easy distribution and collection of results.

Archive and Review Student-Generated Design Stories

Design stories are narratives that are created after solving a particular Design Challenge. These stories follow a specific reflection framework, and discuss several aspects of the design space, such as functional requirements and alternatives to the solutions.
